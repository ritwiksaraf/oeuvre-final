Samvaad 2020



“Ikigai is different for all of us, but one thing we have in common is that we all are searching for its meaning”

-Hector Garcia

"Ikigai" is a beautiful Japanese word that means reason for being. We grow up in a certain ambiance where each person involved in our life has different perspectives and goals. We often find ourselves caught in the web of the question "What about tomorrow?" In the hustle and the influence of the situation, we tend to forget our uniqueness, our reason for being and blindly follow the herd ahead.

Ikigai is the discovery of one's lost inner voice. If happiness had an equation, it would be ikigai. We live in a bubble of hustle, when the pin of introspection bursts the bubble, that's when we discover our ikigai. The journey to find one begins right after answering the four questions: 1. What do I love? 2.What am I good at? 3. What can I be paid for? 4. What does the world need? Life is spontaneous, ikigai is the plan with several hints and clues to guide and help you dodge a bumpy ride. On knowing what our aim is and our strength and weaknesses are, we get an analysis of our life at that particular point and what we need to do to attain a stage of feeling satisfied. This is the guideline to have a happy, content and prosperous long life.

We live in the world where winning is always celebrated, the aspect of not winning is never considered an option raising the anxiousness and hunger to attain the unachievable, often leading to dissatisfaction and depressive moments. Ikigai, the realization that there will be alternative paths to reach destination while enjoying the journey of living comes handy.

Fr.C. Rodrigues Institute of Technology, Vashi truly believes in nurturing the students' intellectual and social skills by conducting events that stimulate the minds of the youth to raise their voices to emphasize their opinions and the ability to listen to every person in the room with their share of perspective.

DAY 1

On 17th and 18th of January 2020, the fourth edition of the annual debate competition SAMVAAD was conducted by Fr. C. Rodrigues Institute of Technology and Agnels School of Law. The inaugural ceremony was held in the seminar hall in the presence of Fr. Almeida, Dr. SM Khot (principal of Fr.C.R.I.T), Mr. Kevin (Principal of Agnels school of law), Mr. Prasad Menon, Mrs. Audrey Correa, the event commenced after lighting of the lamp followed by prayer, magazine release and words of wisdom by Fr. Almeida and principal’s  of both the associated colleges.

The coordinator of the event, Mrs. Audrey Correa briefed the participants about the rules to be followed and the student coordinators gave short insight of the debate. The theme of the debate was- “Science, Technology and Human Rights”, the subjects were assigned to each team a day prior to the event. 18 teams from 15 colleges from Mumbai participated in the event. The debate was conducted in modified oxford format. The teams were assigned rooms on the 4th and 5th floor with a judge in each room.

Day 1 of the event concluded with selection of teams for the semi-final round.

DAY 2

On Day 2 the semi-final and final rounds were conducted with the same vigor and enthusiasm as of Day 1. The motive to organize the event was successful as it provided a platform to students where they can freely express their opinions and overall enhance their public speaking skills.

The winners of SAMVAAD 2020 Sahil Salian and Bhavana Raisinghani from V.E.S.I.T were awarded Rs. 12000 cash and Rs. 2000 worth coupons, 1st Runner ups Snehal Sanjay Adarkar and Aashay from New Law College were awarded Rs. 8000 cash and Rs. 1000 worth coupons, the semi-finalist Manish Sainani and Sourish Gavali were given Rs. 2000 coupons. Sourish Gavali bagged the title of Best Speaker of SAMVAAD 2020.

The fruitful journey of the event was the result of the consistent effort taken by the student and staff coordinators. The participants appreciated the way in which the debate competition was organize
